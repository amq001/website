## What Is Fine-Tuning of AI Models?

Fine-tuning of AI models is the process of taking a pre-trained machine learning or language model and further training it on domain-specific data to improve performance for targeted tasks.

### Pre-Trained vs. Fine-Tuned Models

**Pre-trained models:**
- Trained on large, general datasets  
- Broad knowledge but limited specialization  
- Faster to deploy but less accurate for niche use cases  

**Fine-tuned models:**
- Trained on business-specific data  
- Higher accuracy and contextual understanding  
- Aligned with enterprise requirements and tone  

AI model fine-tuning enables organizations to create custom AI models that understand their industry, customers, and workflows.

## Types of Fine-Tuning

Different business needs require different fine-tuning approaches.

### Full Fine-Tuning

All model parameters are updated during training.

**Best for:**
- Highly specialized domains  
- Maximum performance requirements  

### Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)

Techniques like LoRA and adapters update only a small subset of parameters.

**Advantages:**
- Lower compute costs  
- Faster training  
- Easier updates  

### Instruction Fine-Tuning

Models are trained to follow specific instructions and output formats.

**Ideal for:**
- Chatbots  
- Task-oriented assistants  
- Workflow automation

## FAQs (SEO Optimized)

### What is AI model fine-tuning?

AI model fine-tuning is the process of retraining a pre-trained model on domain-specific data to improve accuracy and relevance for targeted tasks.

### How much does fine-tuning cost?

The cost of fine-tuning depends on data volume, model size, and infrastructure. Parameter-efficient fine-tuning typically reduces costs compared to full fine-tuning.

### How long does fine-tuning take?

Fine-tuning timelines range from a few days for small models to several weeks for large-scale enterprise AI systems.

### Is fine-tuning better than RAG?

Fine-tuning is better for deep domain expertise and consistent behavior, while RAG is ideal for dynamic knowledge retrieval. Many enterprises use both together.
